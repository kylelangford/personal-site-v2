## Version 2: Helpful/Educational

---

**Planning your Drupal 10 to Drupal 11 migration?**

EOL is December 9, 2026. Here's what you need to know:

**What stops working:**
No official security patches after EOL. Your site keeps running, but vulnerabilities won't get fixes.

**What stays the same:**
Drupal 9, 10, and 11 share the same architecture. Most modules only need compatibility updates, not rewrites.

**Realistic timelines:**
- Simple sites: 2-4 weeks
- Complex sites: 8-12 weeks
- Sites with heavy customization: plan extra buffer

**What to do now:**
1. Audit your current modules for D11 compatibility
2. Check your PHP version (D11 requires PHP 8.3+)
3. Review custom code for deprecated APIs
4. Build your timeline backwards from Dec 2026

## Version 3: Thought Leadership/Perspective

---

**Every CMS migration gets delayed. Don't let Drupal 10 → 11 be one of them.**

I've seen this pattern too many times:

1. EOL gets announced
2. Everyone says "we have time"
3. Q3 of the final year: "we should probably start"
4. Q4: panic, rushed timeline, expensive mistakes

Drupal 10 EOL is December 9, 2026.

The migration itself isn't hard—Drupal made 10 → 11 deliberately smooth. Same architecture, compatible modules, incremental upgrade path.

But every organization underestimates:
- Internal review/approval cycles
- QA and testing time
- Holiday code freezes
- The inevitable "while we're at it" scope creep

The companies that do this well start 12-18 months out. Not because the work takes that long, but because organizational reality takes that long.

If you're on Drupal 10 and haven't started planning, now's the time.
